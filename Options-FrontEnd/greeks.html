The Greeks

Quantifying option price sensitivities in the binomial and continuous-time frameworks

Delta

Definition

Delta measures the sensitivity of an option’s value to changes in the price of the underlying asset. It represents the option’s first-order exposure to movements in the underlying.

Mathematical Representation

\[ \Delta = \frac{\partial V}{\partial S} \]

In the binomial model, Delta is approximated by comparing option values across adjacent price nodes.

\[ \Delta \approx \frac{V_{\text{up}} - V_{\text{down}}} {S_{\text{up}} - S_{\text{down}}} \]

Alternate Uses & Interpretation

Hedge Ratio

Delta represents the number of shares of the underlying required to hedge one option position. A Delta of 0.6 implies holding 0.6 shares per option.

Directional Exposure

Delta captures an option’s directional bias. Calls have positive Delta, puts have negative Delta, and the magnitude reflects the option’s moneyness.

Probability Proxy

Under risk-neutral pricing, Delta can be interpreted as an approximation of the probability that the option expires in-the-money.

Gamma

Definition

Gamma measures the sensitivity of Delta to changes in the underlying price. If Delta is velocity, Gamma is acceleration — it determines how fast Delta changes.

Mathematical Representation

\[ \Gamma = \frac{\partial^2 V}{\partial S^2} \]

In a binomial framework, Gamma is approximated using changes in Delta across price nodes.

\[ \Gamma \approx \frac{\Delta_{\text{up}} - \Delta_{\text{down}}} {S_{\text{up}} - S_{\text{down}}} \]

Alternate Uses & Interpretation

Curvature

Gamma captures the curvature of the option’s value. Linear instruments like stocks have zero Gamma; options do not.

Convexity Risk

Long options benefit from large price moves due to positive Gamma, while short options are harmed by volatility.

Re-hedging Frequency

High Gamma means Delta changes rapidly, requiring frequent rebalancing to remain Delta-neutral.

Theta

Definition

Theta measures how an option’s value changes as time passes, holding all else constant. It quantifies time decay.

Mathematical Representation

\[ \Theta = \frac{\partial V}{\partial t} \]

In the binomial model, Theta is approximated using midpoint traversal.

\[ \Theta \approx \frac{V_{\text{mid}} - V_{\text{now}}}{2\,\Delta t} \]

Alternate Uses & Interpretation

Time Decay

As expiration approaches, uncertainty collapses and option value erodes. Theta accelerates near maturity.

Cost of Waiting

Being directionally correct is insufficient — the move must happen fast enough to overcome Theta.

Theta–Gamma Tradeoff

High Gamma positions often come with high Theta decay. Convexity is rarely free.

Vega

Definition

Vega measures sensitivity to volatility. It reflects how option prices respond to changes in uncertainty, not direction.

Mathematical Representation

\[ \text{Vega} = \frac{\partial V}{\partial \sigma} \]

In discrete models, Vega is computed using volatility perturbations.

\[ \text{Vega} \approx \frac{V(\sigma + \varepsilon) - V(\sigma - \varepsilon)} {2\,\varepsilon} \]

Alternate Uses & Interpretation

Volatility Exposure

Vega profits from volatility expansion regardless of direction. Large moves — not correct direction — generate value.

Event Risk

Earnings and macro events inflate implied volatility, creating opportunities — and IV crush risk.

Time Dependence

Longer-dated options have higher Vega since uncertainty needs time to express itself.

Rho

Definition

Rho measures sensitivity to the risk-free interest rate. It quantifies how discounting future payoffs impacts option value.

Mathematical Representation

\[ \rho = \frac{\partial V}{\partial r} \]

In the binomial model, Rho is approximated via rate perturbations.

\[ \rho \approx \frac{V(r+\varepsilon) - V(r-\varepsilon)} {2\,\varepsilon} \]

Alternate Uses & Interpretation

Time Value of Money

Higher interest rates reduce the present value of future payoffs, altering option prices.

Long-Dated Sensitivity

Rho is negligible for short-term options but meaningful for long maturities.

Macro Hedging

Rho exposure is often hedged using rate-sensitive instruments such as bonds, swaps, and futures.
